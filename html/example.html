    Types of Hyperlink

    Hyperlinks are the backbone of the internet, connecting web pages and allowing users to navigate between them. They come in various forms, each serving a different purpose. Here are some of the most common types of hyperlinks:

    • 1
    • 2
    • 3

    Embedded link

    Embedded links are the clickable segments found within text or image content that, when clicked, can lead you to a different webpage webpage altogether, refresh the same window with new content, or even jump to a specific section within the same page.

    Inline link

    Inline links serve as sneak peeks into the broader content, often manifested as thumbnails or preview snippets within an article or a webpage. By clicking on these miniature previews, users are granted access to more detailed content, be it a larger image, a more comprehensive write-up, or additional multimedia resources.

    Hot area

    Hot areas turn static images into interactive experiences, transforming a regular map or graphic into a rich navigable resource. Through image maps with defined hotspots, users can hover over or click on different parts of the image to unveil related information in pop-up windows or navigate to related links.
